Our Philosophy

Food is not
decoration.

Maison Vérité was born from a refusal to perform. No foam. No theatre. No dishes designed for photographs. Just ingredients treated with precision, plated with restraint, and served by people who believe a good meal is still the most intimate thing two strangers can share.

Our kitchen sources from a rotating circle of seven Nigerian farms. Our menu changes with the season, never the trend.

Behind the Kitchen

Chef Adaeze Okonkwo

Trained in Lyon. Returned to Lagos by choice. Adaeze spent six years cooking in Michelin-starred kitchens before deciding that the most interesting ingredients she had ever worked with were the ones her grandmother used without a recipe.

She runs a quiet kitchen. Precise. No shouting. The food shows it.

"I cook Nigerian food the way it deserves — with the same rigour and respect French food gets in Europe."
École Ferrandi, Paris · Le Bernardin, New York · Maison Vérité, Lagos
